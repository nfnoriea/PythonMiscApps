DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
In all of the above named projects, human embryonic stem cells, induced pluripotent stem cells and human mesenchymal stem cells will be expanded and differentiated into osteoblasts, the bone forming cells. The effect of certain compounds (i.e. alcohol, tobacco, nicotine and growth factors) on their differentiation capacity will be tested. In order to do so, differentiation protocols known form mouse cells, will be transferred and tissue markers tested at the gene and protein level. Apoptosis will be measured as well. The same type of experiments will also be performed with primate embryonic stem cells (the common marmoset monkey, Callithrix jacchus and rhesus, Macaca mulata). To control for adequate differentiation capacity, cells will be injected into immnuocompromised mice and teratoma formation assays be be performed. We aim to generate or use genetically modify human and mouse embryonic stem cells and induced pluripotent stem cells to carry fluorescent reporters, overexpress genes or knock-down (out) gene function. These reporter sequences are plasmid based or viral-based and will be transferred into the cells by nucleoporation, chemical transfection techniques or viral infection. Ultimately, these stem cell lines will help us discover the role of signalling pathways in pluripotency (self-renewal) and differentiation, but will also elucidate how stem cells respond to toxicants. We will be able to monitor the behaviour of the stem cells in response to such toxicants by fluorescent microscopy as well as videoimaging. We also study the driving role of microRNAs in cellular differentiation and will perform in utero manipulations of their expression levels. Toxicants will be injected into pregnant dams and embryos assessed for gene/protein expression and skeletal phenotype at specific stages of development.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
Our research will elucidate the molecular pathways, whereby pluripotent stem cells differentiate into osteoblasts. Here, the adult mesenchymal stem cells serve as control cells. In parts of our studies, we will then use this in vitro model to study toxic compounds. The negative effects of these compounds on embryonic development, which ultimately cause malformations, are well described. However, the molecular networks causing this maldevelopment are less well characterized. Using this in vitro model, we may discover molecular pathways, which may be targeted to prevent malformations otherwise caused by these compounds. Objective 1: Generation of reporter stem cell lines Objective 2: Cultivation and differentiation of the reporter stem cell lines Objective 3: Characterizing the response of the reporter stem cell lines to toxicants, chemicals, growth factors and shear
