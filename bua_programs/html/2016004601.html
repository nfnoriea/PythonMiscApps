DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Microglia are the tissue macrophage of the brain and spinal cord. Their activation contributes to several types of neurological disorders. Our studies are aimed at identifying potential points of therapeutic intervention by indentifying specific molecules expressed by microglia. We then test the function of these molecules by artificially increasing and "knocking'down" the expression of specific molecules. Common methods designed to alter expression of molecules within cells are not effective in microglia and many other types of macrophages. Therefore, we use replication incompetent lentiviral vectors to introduce our overexpression or knock-down molecules into microglia.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
To manipulate microglial, macrophage and immune clle gene expression and test for subsequent changes in immune phenotype and function. My lab has identified molecules preferentially expressed in murine microglia (brain macrophages) and/or hematogenously-derived macrophages. The function of these molecules is unknown. Using bioinformatic analysis, we have identified ITIM and ITAM signaling motifs. We do not know if they are active.
