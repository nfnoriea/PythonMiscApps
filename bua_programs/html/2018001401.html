DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The long term goal of this project is to elucidate the molecular mechanism of tissue-specific gene expression. The immediate goals are to determine the mechanism of action of one critical liver-enriched transcription factor, hepatocyte nuclear factor 4a (HNF4a) and other nuclear receptors. Found primarily in the liver, kidney, intestine and pancreas, HNF4a is a highly conserved member of the superfamily of ligand-dependent transcription factors (nuclear receptors) that regulates many target genes essential to basic metabolism as well as xenobiotic and drug metabolism. HNF4a has been linked to several human diseases including hemophilia, diabetes, atherosclerosis and cancer via regulation of hepatitis B viral genes and P450 (cyp) genes. HNF4a is also known to be an essential gene in organisms ranging from insect to man and to be essential for the adult liver phenotype. Despite the obvious importance of HNF4a, much remains to be elucidated about its mechanism of action. To address this, our lab studies various aspects of HNF4-related biology, including interacting proteins, post-translational modifications and responses to environmental signals, ligand and target genes. HNF4a is a member of the superfamily of ligand-dependent transcription factors (nuclear receptors). It interacts with several other receptors and regulates the expression of others. A related project in the lab deals with other nuclear receptors with which we define their DNA binding specificity and target genes. Those receptors are expressed in Cos7 cells and the proteins are extracted and analyzed. Mouse tissues and human cell lines expressing endogenous nuclear receptors are also used.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
To study the mechanism by which the liver-enriched transcription factor HNF4 regulates gene expression. We analyze lipophilic compounds such as fatty acids that bind HNF4, interacting proteins and phosphorylation of HNF4.We also identify the genes that are regulated by HNF4a and the proteins that regulate HNF4a function. To accomplish this we routinely transfect HNF4 and other cDNAs in mammalian and/or insect cell lines and transform HNF4 and other cDNAs in bacteria for expression of protein. We use recombinant adenovirus and lentivirus to express nuclear receptors and other factors in cell lines that are difficult to transfect with plasmids. We inject tumor cells into mice in order to elucidate mechanisms of tumorigenesis. Some of the cell lines we will be injecting will be stably transfected cells in which we are expressing various versions of HNF4 and the Src tyrosine kinase. We will also be looking at the actions of other nuclear receptors by treating animals with their ligands (e.g., dexamethasone for glucocorticoid receptor)and examining changes in gene expression in relevant organs.

Define Controls
Do you need special equipment, engineering safeguards or personal protective equipment to maintain containment of your experiments (hoods, negative pressure rooms, gloves, etc.) True
If “yes”, list your recommendations. We do the work with tissue culture in Biological Safety Cabinets. For routine tissue culture work we use disposable lab coats and gloves. For Adenovirus and lentivirus experiments we use gloves, goggles and disposable lab coats. We will disinfect all non metal equipment and instruments that come in contact with the adenovirus or lentivirus with a solution of 10% bleach or a pharmicidal reagent composed of quartenary benzylammonium compounds. For metal surfaces such as stainless steel hoods the pharmicidal reagent composed of quartenary benzylammonium compounds (available from Pharmacia) will be used. From the MSDS: "It offers a wider spectrum "percentage-kill-ratio" of enveloped and non-enveloped viruses, gram- positive, gram-negative and acid-fast bacteria (as well as spore-forming bacteria) and is effective as well against as fungi (yeasts, mildew, molds) and its spores. It prevents the growth and contamination of Mycoplasma spp. and is effective against a vast array of viruses including HIV and Hepatitis B." UV irradiation for at least 20 minutes after each use of the hood will also be used. We will double bag all materials that are autoclaved and disinfect the exterior of the autoclaved bag with bleach before disposal. We will use a biosafety cabinet with a UV light.
