DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
My research is in Agricultural Plant Genomics. My research goals are unified by the question, "What are the fundamental biochemical processes within which there exists genetic diversity related to tolerance of abiotic stresses such as low temperature, drought, and salinity and resistance to pests and pathogens?" The plants that I study using rDNA molecules are cowpea and barley. The work includes the use of fragments of the barley and cowpea genomes, as well as study of these fragments to infer the function of the proteins they encode and their relationship to plant traits. The presence of these fragments in a bacterial host provides a convenient source of DNA molecules to enable these studies. All of the work with these molecules is under the umbrella of my Hatch project.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
Recombinant DNA methods have been used to produced bacterial artificial chromosome (BAC) clones of barley and cowpea, which will be used to define the specific genes or positions in a DNA molecule that are responsible for traits that have been observed at the whole plant level. Existing collections of barley and cowpea BACs will be used in transient expression assays and examined using restriction mapping to address these objectives. In cowpea a goal is to identify a gene controlling pigment production in sepals, pods and seed coats. Other genes controlling chilling tolerance, heat tolerance, drought tolerance, and resistance to several insects, fungi and viruses are also possible topics. In barley a goal is to explore environmentally induced unequal recombination in a region containing stress-induced genes known as dehydrins.
