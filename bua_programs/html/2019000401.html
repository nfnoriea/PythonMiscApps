DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Our Research Our research focuses on developing biological and technological tools to dissect the molecular events driving apicomplexan parasites with a particular interest in malaria parasite life cycle progression. Using functional genomics approaches, we expect to elucidate critical regulatory networks driving the parasite life cycles and identify novel therapeutic strategies. There are three main research projects ongoing in the laboratory 1. Revealing mechanisms controlling gene expression in the Apicomplexan parasites Mechanisms controlling gene expression in Apicomplexan parasites are still poorly understood. Rising evidences indicate that control of gene expression in the malaria parasites occurs at multiple levels, one of those being chromatin remodeling. It is increasingly apparent that histone turnover and histone post-translational modifications are important in chromatin structure and transcriptional regulation. Determination of chromatin’s structural changes will therefore be critical for the understanding of transcriptional regulation in Apicomplexan parasites including human malaria parasites . Using genome wide complementary approaches; we are investigating the parasite dynamic chromatin structure and the molecular components that regulate their features. 2. Drug discovery and natural products In addition to fundamental scientific approaches, we are developing drug-screening assays and high content live cell confocal imaging technologies to identify small molecule inhibitors and their morphological effects on the Apicomplexan parasites. Ongoing collaborations with several institutes are providing us with a comprehensive array of natural products. So far we have already uncovered several compounds that can inhibit malaria growth in the low nano molar ranges. Experiments to identify drug targets and drug mechanism of actions are still ongoing. 3 – Detection of small interfering RNAs (siRNAs) in mosquitoes to improve surveillance of emerging mosquito borne diseases in California. The most recent project developed in my lab is designed to develop a new genome wide technology to detect infectious diseases in mosquitoes. We want to detect in a genome-wide manner “old and new” infectious agents to prevent widespread of human disease transmitted by mosquitoes. This innovative work is done in collaboration with Mosquito and Vector Control Districts and is funded through the CDC to the Pacific Southwest Regional Center of Excellence for Vector-Borne Disease Research at the University of California (UCR and UCD). Note that that my lab only received total RNA extracted from mosquitoes in approved facilities. We do not grow or collect mosquitoes in my lab.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
1- Development of new biological tools to dissect the molecular events driving Apicomplexan parasites 2- Discovery of new targets and therapeutic approaches for the treatment of Apicomplexan parasites. 3- Improve surveillance of emerging mosquito borne diseases in California.
