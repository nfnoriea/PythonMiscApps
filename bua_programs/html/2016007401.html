DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Current evidence strongly suggests that the interplay between the prefrontal cortex and hippocampus is critical for learning and memory. The central goal of this project is to define the role of the mPFC neural network in fear memory specificity and determine the functional relations between the mPFC and hippocampus in the generation and retrieval of distinct memories that are resistant to confusion. Our main hypothesis is that the mPFC is critical for the integration of information necessary for learning subtle differences between temporally separated relevant and non-relevant stimuli. My laboratory at UCR has developed a new genetic methodology to target neurons in the prefrontal cortex and manipulate selectively their role in a type of synaptic plasticity underlying long-term memory consolidation without disconnecting them from the circuit and diminishing their abilities to support working memory, attention, information acquisition, and short-term memory. The procedure involves virus-mediated gene delivery facilitated with stereotactic survival surgery to different brain regions such as prefrontal cortex, hippocampus and thalamus. Mutant mice are tested using behavioral, electrophysiological and histological techniques to define the role of prefrontal neuronal circuits in memory. The proposed research is relevant to impaired cognitive function caused by maladaptations in neural circuitry. These type of neural maladaptations of neural circuits are likely to be associated with neural processes underlying post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) or developmental disorders such as Rubinstein-Taybi syndrome (RTS) and schizophrenia.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
we use viruses to deliver genes to specific cell types in mouse brains regions in order to manipulate neural activity and cognitive functioning.

If “yes”, describe the molecules, including genetic maps and the fraction of virus genome present, and safety precautions. We use recombinant viruses: HSV1, AAV1/2 and AAV8. Viral sequences present in the recombinant AAV and recombinant HSV-1 viruses encode the ability for viruses to enter cells. The genes encoding ability to replicate are not present in recombinant viruses used in our laboratory. The recombinant AAV virus and the recombinant HSV-1 virus are deriving from their wild type form of virus by removing the viral genes (critical for virus assembly and replication) and replacing them with the gene under the study referred to as the transgene. Thus, the recombinant AAV virus and the recombinant HSV-1 virus are both unable to grow without appropriate helper virus. However these recombinant viruses are capable to enter cells such as neurons. After the recombinant virus (AAV or HSV-1) enters cells, the transgene expression is directed by provided transcriptional regulatory system.
