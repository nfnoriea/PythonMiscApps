DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
We construct optical nano-particles containing near infrared dyes such as indocyanine green. The encapsulating material can be a polymer (polyallyl(amine) hydrochloride amine), proteins derived from the capsid of the plant-infecting brome mosaic virus, or constructs derived from human, mouse, or bovine erythrocytes. We investigate the ability of these constructs in cell imaging, and photo-destruction of cells. We evaluate the interaction of these particles with various cell lines to study cytotoxicity and examine their targeting potentials. These cells are human embryonic kidney cells, human endothelial cells, normal and cancerous human bronchial epithelial and cervical epithelial cells, human ovarian and breast cancer cells. We are also interested in the interaction of our various types of our constructs with cells of the reticuloendothelial system (RES) to understand the effects of the constructs materials on the resulting biodistribution. For these studies, we use human blood monocytes, human hepatocytes, human spleen macrophages, mouse kupffer cells, and mouse spleen macrophages, which are important constituents of the RES system.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
(1) Optical imaging of cells with and without incubation with various constructs; (2) Laser irradiation of cells incubated by with and without various constructs.
