DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The overall goal is to design and fabricate biomaterial scaffolds for bone, cartilage and neural regeneration. Candidate materials include biodegradable metals (e.g. magnesium alloys, iron alloys), graphene, collagen hydrogels and composites. Additional surface coatings can improve cellular adhesion and control biomaterial degradation. Cellular biocompatibility, cytotoxicity, and stem cell differentiation will be determined using both stem cells and differentiated cell types. This research is essential to verify the biocompatibility of a scaffold prior to conducting any in vivo experiments. We will be obtaining fresh human blood to test the hemocytocompatability of new materials. A circulation flow device will be developed for this test. We will be testing E. coli (ATTC 25922) and other non pathogenic bacteria (Enterobacter aerogenes ATCC 35028,Bacillus subtilis ATCC 7060, Serratia marcescens ATCC 8195, S. Epidermidis), Pseduomonas aeruginosa (ATCC 29260), Staphylococcus aureus(ATCC 25923), Methicillin Resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA, ATCC 33591) with biomaterials as part of a study on resorbable implant materials for urilogical applications. We will be testing microorganisms, such as Candida albican, Candida glabrata, and their drug-resistant strains on biomaterials developed.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
The specific objective to work with biohazardous materials (i.e. cells, cadaveric tissues, proteins) is to investigate how they interact with the designed biomaterial scaffolds in vitro. This work will provide feedback on how to design better biomaterials to improve tissue regeneration. Human cell lines will be utilized to closely examine how specific cells may react with the scaffolds. We do not use recombinant methods or transgenic organisms. Our cells are established cell lines from ATCC and established stem cells at UCR Stem cell core. The objective is to test bacterial and microbial response to novel antimicrobial biomaterials for biomedical applicaitons. Non pathogenic and pathogenic bacteria and yeast will be used.
