DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
To defend themselves against microbial attack, plants have evolved a highly effective immune system. Recognition of pathogenic microorganisms induces coordinated up-regulation of a multitude of defense genes. We are interested in regulatory mechanisms controlling such extensive transcriptional re-programming in the model plant Arabidopsis thaliana (Arabidopsis). Using micro-arrays we identified several groups of genes responding in a highly coordinated manner to pathogen recognition. The pathogen we use for such experiments is the Oomycete Pernospoa parasitica. Applying a variety of different methods we want to identify proteins (transcription factors) responsible for coordinated defense gene expression in Arabidopsis. The planned approaches will involve use of transgenic Arabidopsis lines plants transformed with reporter genes as well as with T-DNA insertions in genes of interest. These transgenic lines will be infected with spores of Hyalopersonospora arabidopsidis.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
1.) transgenic Arabidopsis lines transformed with reporter genes will be used to identify and test putative cis-elements that are required for defense gene expression. We will test sequence motifs that are conserved in promoters of co-regulated genes (such conserved motifs may constitute cis-elements common to these genes). We will also fuse promoters of representative members of such gene groups to reporter genes and identify important cis-elements by deletion analysis of these reporter genes in transgenic Arabidopsis plants. 2.) Transgenic Arabidopsis lines with T-DNA insertions will be used to test genes that are potentially involved in defense gene regulation. T-DNAs are relative large DNA stretches from Argobacgterium tumefaciens. They can randomly insert into plant genomic DNA and disrupt genes. There are several collections of Arabidopsis mutants with T-DNA insertions in defined genes from the SALK institute or Syngenta. Lines with insertions in putative regulatory genes that showed interesting expression patterns in our micro array experiments will be tested for their roles in the plant immune system using such T-DNA mutants. 3.) Hyaloperonospora arabidopsidis will be used as a stimulus for above mentioned reporter gene assyas as well as for testing T-DNA insertion mutants for a reduction in their defense capability. 4.) chemicals that induce expression of H. arabidopsidis responsive reporter genes will be tested to deterime if they induce plant immune responses.
