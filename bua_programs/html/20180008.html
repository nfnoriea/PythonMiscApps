DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
1. Our lab uses in vitro cell culture systems and in vivo animal model systems to study chemokines functions in a number of physiological processes such as inflammation, wound healing and angiogenesis. For our in vitro work, we study the signal transduction mechanisms of the chemokine production with our cell culture system. Plasmids encoding luciferase-based reporters will be transfected by liposome-based method into cultured cells and the luciferase activity present in the cell extracts will be measured. We also use animal model to perform in vivo experiments. Cancer cells will be used to study cell and molecular biological aspects of cancer growth and progression of prostate cancer. 2. "Bacteria collected from mouse wounds, isolated and cultured by Borneman lab, will be applied to mouse wounds to examine activity of individual/combinations of bacterial species. Wounds will be monitored/photographed and tissues collected". 3. animals with the bacteria will only be handled by lab members (not vivaria staff). Imaging will be done in the vivaria room. The animals will be brought to the lab to collect tissues. During transportation they will be placed in a large cart and covered with a trap as approved by the IACUC in my animals protocol. The carcasses will be put in plastic bags and then transported to the vivarium in a secondary container for disposal. For the collection of the wound tissue we will uncover the wounds and immediately apply fixative to kill the bacteria in place and dispose of the tegaderms in the bio-hazard container to be autoclaved 4. We will also be using human placental tissue to cover the wound and determine whether and how they help wound healing. Animals will be treated in the vivarium with placental membranes using the biosafety cabinet. Treated animals will not be taken out of the vivarium until euthanasia.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
1. Various constructs containing promoter-driven luciferase gene will be used in our cultured cells as a reporter to study signal transduction of cheomkine transcriptional regulation. But the cells containing recombinant DNA will be used/killed by procedure or the left-over autoclaved, thus leaving no threats to environment. Cancer cells will be used in culture to look for genes and biological processes that are involved in adhesion and cell migration. 2. Activity of specific bacterial species will be examined during wound healing
