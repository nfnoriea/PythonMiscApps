DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
We are investigating the soil, plant and animal microbiome in general. In particular, we are working with soils and dusts with no known pathogens, collected in US field locations, non-exotic terrestrial gastropods with no known pathogens, sterile DNA extracts from native and exotic plants and soils. The purpose is to characterize how plants mediate soil microbiomes and vice versa, as well as to understand what microorganisms disperse via dust. We are also working with DNA extracts and the supernatant of the lung BAL (bronchioalveolar lavage) from mice, born and raised under laboratory conditions. We are exposing the mice in a chamber in Dr. Lo's lab to dust from regions of CA to look for the lung microbiome responses. The purpose is to characterize how exposure to dust changes the lung microbiome of the mice.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
The goals are to understand the soil, plant and animal microbiome. Specifically, to understand how soil microorganisms may be transported from one location to another using plants, animals and wind-borne dust as vectors, as well as the impact of soil microbial diversity on the ecosystem functions the soil provides. Another specific objective is the characterize the interaction between lung inflammatory response and microbiome.
