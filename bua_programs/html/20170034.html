DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
RNAi and related small RNAs were originally identified in C. elegans two decades ago, and later found as highly conserved pathways, which play critical roles in animals and plants. The proposed studies will use an integrated approach of biochemistry, genetics and bioinformatics to identify proteins involved in the biogenesis of small RNAs, and investigate their roles in gene regulation in both C. elegans and mammalian cells.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
The proposed research will use recombinant DNA. One purpose is to express proteins, usually tool enzymes from C. elegans, E. coli, and phage, in E. coli cells, thus saving money for purchasing the commercial enzymes. And the other purpose is to study the functions of C. elegans proteins involved in small RNA biogenesis. Basically, we will purify the recombinant C. elegans proteins from E. coli and study their functions in vitro. The third purpose is to do CRYSPR in C. elegans to knockout and knockin genes. Therefore, we can tag the target proteins in vivo to do IP, and to investigate their functions. In addition, we will perform mammalian cell culture work using Crispr and recombinant DNA.
