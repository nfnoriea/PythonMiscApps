DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Characterization of RNA interference (RNAi) as an antiviral defense mechanism in plants, mice and in cultured human cells; identification and mechanism analysis of the host genes in the defense pathway and of the viral suppressors of the plant and animal antiviral response; determine if a new vaccine strategy against animal viral pathogens could be developed according to the RNAi-mediated antiviral immunity; and application of these mechanistic insights in the control of plant viral diseases.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
Infectious cDNA clones of Coxsackievirus B3 (CVB3), Dengue virus serotype 2 and 4 are used to make the low/non virulent RNAi suppressor-deletion mutants as vaccine candidate strains for mouse challenges. Plasmid vectors based the backbones of Adeno-associated virus and Murine leukemia virus are for the expression of specific human genes (such as Argonaute and Dicer proteins) or reporter (such as GFP or luciferase) in cultured mammalian cells or mice. Vaccine strains of influenza A viruses and dengue viruses are used to determine the role of RNAi and of viral suppressors of RNA silencing during infection. The negative-strand RNA viruses Vesicular stomatitis virus and Sendai virus will be used for cell culture and mouse infection to the role of RNAi during mammalian infection. In addition, cucumber mosaic virus, citrus tristeza virus, turnip yellow mosaic virus, tomato yellow leaf curl virus and related plant viruses as well as infectious cDNA clones of these viruses will be used.

Are you using human or primate cells in animals? False
Are you infecting animals with infectious agents? True
If “yes,” describe any potential environmental or health hazards, containment precautions, and disposal protocols. Nodamura virus is rapidly cleared in mice 21 days or older after birth, is virulent only to suckling mice, and is not known to spread in mouse colonies or to other animals. Thus, no special precaution is necessary. AAV particles produced after co-transfection with the helper plasmids are for single-cycle infection only and the mice injected with these AAV particles do not produce infectious virus for secondary infection and thus do not have environmental or health hazards. Vaccine strains of human influenza A virus cause weight loss in adult mice, but they are not transmissible between mice.
Do the animals shed the infectious agents? True
If “yes,” how long will shedding of the infectious agent occur? Shedding of Vesicular stomatitis virus from tonsils is known to occur in pigs. Transmission of Sendai virus among mice is by direct contact or contaminated fomites.
