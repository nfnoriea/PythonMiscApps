DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
To clone and characterize the structure, organization and expression of plant peptidases, and defense response genes. To determine the role of these proteins in plant defense and development. To determine insect responses to different plant defense environments. To develop protocols for Bemisia tabaci B transformation in collaboration with the Atkinson lab.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
The project will use transgenic tomato, tobacco, alfalfa, cassava and/or Arabidopsis plants that express plant or insect sequences as over-expression, antisense, RNAi or CRISPR constructs. T-DNA tagged insertional mutants will be used. We will over-express plant and/or insect proteins or plant-reporter fusion proteins in planta. Proteins will be over-expressed in E. coli and/or yeast and characterized biochemically. In collaboration with Peter Atkinson (ENT), we will microinject B. tabaci MEAM1 (B) eggs with reporter genes and constructs to create genetic tools for manipulating the whitefly genome. CRISPR-Cas9 injections will also be performed. The Walling lab will rear insects for microinjection and rear putative transgenic insects in the IQF.
