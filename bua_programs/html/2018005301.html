DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Research in the Nair lab is focused on understanding the function of macrophages recruited to mucosal sites following infection and how they regulate host immunity and inflammation. Specific areas of research include investigating the function of alternatively activated macrophages in helminth infection-induced lung and gut inflammation. Additionally, the lab employs models of intestinal inflammation including bacterial infection and chemical induced injury to investigate what cell-types and cell-derived factors are important to regulate this response. An additional avenue of research in collaboration with Dr Rong Hai will be to understand the importance of the host immune response to Influenza and Zika virus infection.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
To understand how the host immune response combats pathogenic infections as well as regulate dangerous inflammatory responses, we employ murine models of infection and injury-induced inflammation. This includes using infectious agents such as helminths (Nippostrongylus, Trichinella, Trichuris, Heligmosomoides), bacteria (Salmonella, Listeria, Citrobacter), parasites (Toxoplasma), and viruses (Zika, Influenza) and chemicals (dextran sodium sulfate). Zika virus- specific information: Investigating ZKIV replication will enable us to understand the differential replication kinetics of ZIKV in mouse immune cells. This study supplies vital information for further understanding its replication characters. In addition, it supplied an important tool for evaluating efficacy of screening antivirals. E.coli-induced sepsis. To identify new therapeutics for sepsis, we propose a mouse sepsis model. Mice will be infected with intraperitoneal injection of E. coli (ATCC 25922) at 2-20x107 cfu. The E. coli strain is designated biosafety level 1, and standard CDC guidelines will be followed to ensure other mice are not infected.
