DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The plant project work is aimed at development of molecular markers for and genomic mapping of important agronomic traits, especially disease and drought resistance, in cotton, cowpea, and carrot. The intent is to develop marker-assisted selection tools for plant breeders to conduct indirect selection for genetic improvement of crops. Depending on availability of funds, we plan to establish a working VIGS (virus induced gene silencing)system in cowpea for functional analysis of candidate resistance genes. The nematode project work is to develop DNA profiles for nematode isolates that vary in virulence to specific host plants. Identified polymorphisms will be assessed for linkage to virulence traits and for tracking cross-fertilization success in genetic crosses between nematode isolates.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
Development of molecular markers for and genomic mapping of important agronomic traits, especially nematode, disease, insect and drought resistance, in cotton, cowpea, and carrot. We will develop and or use existing SSR, SNP and other genome sequence-derived DNA marker development and genomic mapping of traits. Available cotton, cowpea and carrot genomic DNA BAC libraries and whole-genome sequence assemblies will be used to identify candidate genes associated with trait determinants. Selected BACs will be completely sequenced, the sequences assembled and annotated, and used for identifying markers for mapping and candidate resistance gene locations. RNA expression profiling will be made using pathogen infected and noninfected root samples to identify genes of importance in the resistance reaction incited by infection or drought. Candidate R genes will be tested for function by use of VIGS (virus-induced gene silencing) in cowpea, using RNA virus vectors commonly used for VIGS in soybean - Apple Latent Spherical Virus, Bean Pod Mottle Virus, Cucumber Mosaic Virus). The nematode work will develop SSR and SNP profiles for nematode isolates that vary in virulence to specific host plants. Identified polymorphisms will be assessed for linkage to virulence traits and for tracking cross-fertilization success in genetic crosses between nematode isolates. The objective is to study the behavior of nematode (a)virulence genes in populations exposed to R gene selection pressure on resistant plants.
