DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The objectives of these projects are to analyze plant genomes (mainly Citrus and asparagus) in studies of genetic diversity, evolution, and gene function. This research involves cloning genomic DNA, cDNA, and PCR products in appropriate vectors and analysis of the cloned DNA. Specific projects include BAC libraries, production of cDNA and genomic DNA libraries, and cloning PCR products for sequence analysis. Small genomic segments of DNA of the citrus bacterial pathogen Candidatus Liberibacter asiaticus (CLas) and related species may be cloned in E. coli and these noninfectious clones used as controls for copy number estimates by qPCR. Samples of dead citrus tissue or Asian citrus psyllids that may contain dead CLas are obtained Florida under APHIS permit. No work with live CLas bacteria. Populations of HLB resistant and susceptible novel hybrids generated from crossing Citrus with Australian citrus relatives will be analyzed to understand genomic differences and to correlate resistance with genomic variations. Citrus genes believed responsible for susceptibility to CLas will be edited to silence expression (all testing for HLB tolerance will be conducted in BSL-3P facilities at UCD or in Riverside under separate BUA. Cloned DNA of asparagus viruses is used as a control for detection methods. Mexican lime plants expressing FT from Arabidopsis thaliana under control of NOS promoter will be tested to reduce juvenility in citrus hybrids.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
Biohazardous materials are used to produce sufficient quantities of specific DNA sequences for subsequent analysis, while avoiding PCR-induced errors. Only cloning procedures can produce materials suitable for analysis of certain DNA sequences. Gene editing will be conducted to create citrus potentially resistant to HLB. The transgenic Mexican limes will be used to reduce juvenility in citrus hybrids which will be grafted to the Mexican lime.
