DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
I will be working with 3 plant pathogenic bacteria, Xylella fastidiosa, Pantoea stewartii subsp. stewartii and Spiroplasma citri. The Xylella fastidiosa strains are strains that have been isolated in California. Pantoea stewartii susbsp stewartii is found throughout North America and is a pathogen on sweet corn and maize but does not pose a major threat to these crops. The particular strains I will have in my lab have originated from a lab at the University of Connecticut. In the cases of X. fastidiosa and P. stewartii I plan to make targeted gene knockouts in genes that are putatively involved in virulence. These mutants will be tested in various lab assays including gene expression, attachment and biofilm formation. Mutants in both organisms will also be tested for virulence in their host plants either in the greenhouse or growth chamber. Additionally, insect transmission experiments will be conducted that test the ability of sharpshooter insect vectors to transmit certain mutant strains of Xylella fastidiosa to grapevines under controlled greenhouse conditions.I also have a Xylella fastidiosa project where we will clone and recombinantly express cell wall degrading enzyme genes found in X. fastidiosa. In addition, I have a citrus project where we isolate fungal and bacterial endophytes from citrus collected in California and potentially Florida and Texas. The Spiroplasma citri strain will be transformed with a recombinant vector expressing a modified Bt toxin to test for toxicity of this toxin to the asian citrus psyllid. The Pseudomonas fluorescens strain we will work with is not a plant pathogen. We are testing its abilities to act as a plant beneficial bacterium that can be developed into a biological control organism for diseases caused by Xylella fastidiosa and Huanglongbing.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
The specific objectives of this research is to elucidate the host/pathogen interactions of both Xylella fastidiosa and Pantoea stewartii subsp. stewartii with their respective hosts. We also will use Spiroplasma citri to introduce a recombinant Bt toxin to the asian citrus psyllid. We are also testing potential microbial biocontrol agents.
