DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The goal of this project is to improve plant drought tolerance by using genetic and chemical approaches. Transgenic plants will be constructed using Agrobacterium mediated transformation. These plants will be analyzed for alterations in growth and development and abiotic stress tolerance. Recombinant DNA will be made and used for protein expression in non-pathogenic E. coli. Other standard molecular biological tools will be employed for constructing plasmids and other recombinant DNAs for routine research. These experiments do not involve hazardous DNAs or pathogenic organisms, with the exception of Agrobacterium (as described above). The primary transgenic selectable markers used for construction of transgenic plants are glufosinate, kanamycin and hygromycin resistances genes. The transgenic plants created in our lab often co-express a green fluorescent protein (GFP) reporter protein or modified abscisic acid receptor proteins.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
To better understand the growth and development of plants; to engineer abiotic stress tolerance in plants using ABA receptor technology.

Are you using hosts or vectors of rDNA (excluding E. coli K-12) that are viable pathogens of animals, plants, or microbes? True
If “yes”, describe any potential environmental or health hazards, containment precautions, and disposal protocols. Agrobacterium rhizogenes and related species (Agrobacterium radiobacter, Sinorhizobium, and Xanthomonas axonopodis) can cause root mats on many plants. We will use a biosafety cabinet to contain the bacteria when conducting infections, grow infected plant material under otherwise sterile / isolated conditions and use appropriate sterile microbiological technique when culturing the Agrobacterium. We will autoclave all waste contaminated with Agrobacterium.
