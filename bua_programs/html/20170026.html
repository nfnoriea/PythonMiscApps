DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Our laboratory studies genetic mechanisms of development in the nematode, C. elegans, as well as other non-pathogenic nematodes that are related to C. elegans. We also examine the function of cells in differentiated tissues of C. elegans and related nematodes. Recently we are exploring expression of genes in the pathogenic nematode Steinernema that infects some insect larvae. We will propagate the nematodes on agar plates supplemented with Xenorhabdus bacteria.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
Recombinant DNA technology will be used for the following purposes: 1. To generate novel fusion genes that will be introduced into nematodes. 2. To generate constructs that will be introduced into E. coli for the purpose of expressing and purifying recombinant proteins. 3. To propagate segments of nematode genes in E. coli for the purposes of sequencing or generation of RNA. 4. To propagate segments of genes that will be used as intermediates to generate other recombinant DNA. 5. To use and generate reagents that can cause directed modification of DNA in nematode chromosomes. 6. To test C. elegans developmental mutant strains for their ability to grow on Pseudomonas aeruginosa bacteria, a commonly used species to study C. elegans immunity.

E. coli XL1-Blue, HT115(DE3), NEB10-beta, and OmniMAX 2 T1R(Thermo Scientific) are made resistant to antibiotics through transformation with recombinant plasmids. Strains OP50 and HT115(DE3) are used for cultivation of Caenorhabditis nematodes. The host strains used do not pose a health risk for workers.
