DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Genes relevant to the biology of oomycete plant pathogens will be studied. The primary target of these analyses is Phytophthora infestans, which causes late blight of potato and tomato. Limited studies will be performed of related Phytophthora spp, such as P. capsci , P cinnamomi, P. sojae (aall plant pathogens)), and Pythium ultimum (a saprophyte and opportunistic plant pathogen). Genes that will be studied are those that are involved in (i) the formation and germination of the spores, (ii) nutrient acquisition and metabolism, (iii) host specificity, and (iv) fungicide resistance. The long-term goals include understanding the evolution of oomycetes, genetic diversity within each species, and identifying potential targets for crop protection chemicals.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
1. Sequences from Phytophthora will be cloned in E. coli for the purposes of DNA sequencing, or for the construction of plasmids for oomycete transformation. 2. Sequences from Phytophthora will be cloned in E. coli or yeast (Pichia pastoris or Saccharoymces cerevisiae) species to express recombinant proteins, or for protein-protein interaction studies. 3. Sequences from Phytophthora spp. will be transformed into P. infestans and relatives for the purposes of silencing the native genes; for expressing higher levels of the relevant proteins; for expressing native and artificial microRNAs; and for expressing fusion proteins with tags such as green fluorescent protein or epitope tags or promoters fused to such reporters. The CRISPR system will also be expressed in Phytophthora spp. for gene knockout and replacement studies. None of the experiments are designed to intentionally increase the host range or virulence of the Phytophthora spp. Most experiments should have no effect of virulence or reduce virulence.
