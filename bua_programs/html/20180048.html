DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The objective of the proposal is to determine basic physiology and biochemistry of insect species, both pest and non-pest. The longterm objective is to develop more effective means of controlling insect pests of humans and those in agriculture
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
The projects specific objectives are to determine the function of proteins involved in insect development, to identify bacterial toxin sequences, and to elucidate proteins involved in toxin interaction. For functional studies some clones will be expressed in E coli, Bacillus thuringiensis, Bacillus megaterium, Paraclostridium bifermantans, Saccharomyces cerevisae, Drosophila melanogaster and Aedes aegypti. Mutant Drosophila and Aedes will also be used for in vivo functional analysis. For expression of clones in E coli, Bacillus, Paraclostridium, and Saccharomyces we use routine plasmid based transformation. One project involves the characterization of mechanisms of action of mosquito active toxins from Paraclostridium bifermentans. These toxins are not toxic to vertebrates and most invertebrates. We hypothesize they act on the nervous system of only specific mosquito species, for example Anopheles but not Aedes species. For these experiments we need to use a positive control that utilizes Botulinum neurotoxins A and B to cleave the human targets, which are expressed in E. coli and purified. The amounts of Botulinum neurotoxins A and B needed are in ug amounts, and they are available commercially. For creating mutant Drosophila we use P-element based transformation and use mutant flies created by stock centers and other labs, including RNAi and UAS-GAL4 lines. For creating mutant Aedes aegypti we will use two approaches. The first is piggybac mediated transformation to drive genes under an inducible promoter (e.g. Aedes heat shock) or tissue specific promoters (e.g. an Aedes ALP expressed in larval gut); allowing the use of RNAi and UAS-GAL4 in Aedes. We also plan to use Crispr/Cas9 to label genes of interest; e.g. use one of GFP/CFP/Tomato to label the N- or C-terminus of genes of interest. We also plan to use Crispr/Cas9 to knockout genes on interest. None of the approaches used in my lab will result in gene drives.
