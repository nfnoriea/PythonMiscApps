DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
This project is focused on elucidating the mechanism of certain steps in the synthesis of plant cell wall polymers. Specifically, the purpose is to find genes that encode enzymes that attach O-methyl ether groups to certain sugars in cell wall polysaccharides. The model system for this work is the moss Physcomitrella patens, which produces 3-O-methyl rhamnosyl residues in the arabinogalactan proteins in its cell wall. The principal approach used in this research has been to make bioinformatic-based guesses for the encoding gene and then transgenically express these candidate moss genes in tobacco. Thus far, this approach has not identified the target rhamnosyl 3-O-methyltransferase but has identified two moss genes that encode galactosyl 3-O-methyltransferases which produce 3-O-methyl galactosyl residues in the cell walls of the transgenic tobacco. Principal focus is now on characterizing these galactosyl 3-O-methyltransferases, while still attempting to identify the rhamnosyl 3-O-methyltransferase. In addition to continuing study of the transgenic tobacco plants, further information on the function of genes will be sought by knocking out the genes in the moss. Knowledge gained about the moss genes and enzymes may lead to future progress on other O-methyltransferases in higher plants with the goal of developing cell wall materials with improved properties for biofuels production.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
The work with biohazardous materials in this project falls in the category of recombinant DNA. The specific objectives of this project are: (1) Identify the gene that encodes an O-methyltransferase that produces 3-O-methyl rhamnosyl residues in the arabinogalactan proteins of the moss Physcomitrella patens. (2) Test the hypothesis that the two galactosyl 3-O-methyltransferases thus far identified are dual function enzymes that can produce either 3-O-methyl galactosyl residues or 3-O-methyl rhamnosyl residues, depending upon substrates and conditions. (3) Generate, and characterize the cell wall properties of, transgenic Nicotiana tabacum plants that express Physcomitrella cell wall methyltransferases. (4) Generate, and characterize the cell wall properties of, Physcomitrella plants in which cell wall methyltransferase genes have been knocked out, or disabled. (5) Use the Physcomitrella cell wall methyltransferase sequences as probes in bioinformatic searches to find other cell wall methyltransferases in model angiosperms; in seed crops that produce considerable residual biomass, such as maize, wheat, barley, sorghum, rice, and other cereals; and in proposed bioenergy crops that produce high biomass yield such as Populus, switchgrass, and Miscanthus. Note that the project proposes only bioinformatic work on the species listed in specific objective (5). The recombinant DNA work on plant species in the project involves only Physcomitrella patens and Nicotiana tabacum.
