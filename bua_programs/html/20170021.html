DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Establishment of proper connections in the developing brain is essential for perception, language, thought, consciousness, learning and memory. Accurate cell migration, axon guidance, synapse formation and subsequent pruning of exuberant neuronal projections are indispensable for the development of a functional nervous system. Disruption of any of these processes can result in a range of neurodevelopmental disorders including schizophrenia, autism, epilepsy, and neuronal degeneration. We have previously identified several genes that participate in neural circuit assembly and refinement. Using a variety of transgenic mice and surgical manipulations, our studies seek to unravel the biological function of these specific genes during normal nervous system development, as well as their contribution to neurodevelopmental disorders.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
The main objective of this study is understanding the molecular events that drive the assembly and refinement of neuronal circuits; these in turn will inform us about the basis of neural pathologies. We will use recombinant DNA, and replication deficient adeno-associated viruses and lentiviruses to explore the molecular underpinings of neural development. The use of these tools will be an essential part of our work.

Recombinant Plasmid DNA, E. coli and Replication deficient AAV: They are Biosafety Level 1 agents and represents a basic level of containment that relies on standard microbiological practices with no special primary or secondary barriers recommended. Replication deficient Lentiviruses and human cell lines: they are Biosafety Level 2 agents, and as such, represent hazards primarily related to accidental percutaneous, mucous membrane, non-intact skin exposures, or ingestions of infectious materials. Extreme caution with contaminated needles or sharp instruments is emphasized. Even though these organisms routinely manipulated at BSL-2 are not known to be transmissible by the aerosol route, procedures with aerosol or high splash potential that may increase risk of such exposure are conducted in primary containment equipment, such as a biosafety cabinet.
