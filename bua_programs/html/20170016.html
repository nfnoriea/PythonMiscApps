DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The purpose of this research is to determine the mechanisms of signal transduction and gene regulation that underlie plant response to non-optimal growth conditions. The studies are aimed at elucidation of cellular mechanisms of signal transduction and gene regulation that mediate changes in physiology and development. The project includes development of plants that are better able to survive abiotic stress. The project outcome provides information on fundamental aspects of cell biology that may have relevance to agriculture, plant biotechnology or human health.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
Recombinant bacterial strains and plants are produced for specific purposes. Recombinant bacteria are used for gene cloning. Recombinant plants are used to address specific questions about plant biology. CRISPR/Cas9 genome editing in rice, maize and Arabidopsis to alter promoters, protein coding regions, RNA regulatory elements and to knock-out gene function, of one or multiple related genes. As is the standard practice for these plants, the editing machinery will be delivered on a T-DNA via agrobacterium. These edited plants will be used to address specific questions about plant biology; we address fundamental questions about development and response to abiotic stress. Genome editing will be performed to make stable (heritable gene mutations and also to perform cell-specific mutations, if feasible (non-heritable). We will also use genome edited lines produced by a collaborator at Iowa State University (Bing Yang) in a NSF funded project.
