DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The purpose of the project is to determine the effects of nematode venom toxins. To do this, we will be injecting flies with the purified venom toxins. To help us understand the role of the toxins in the infection, we will be injecting flies that are already sick with one of a number of bacterial pathogens: E. coli, Listeria monocytogenes, Salmonella typhimurium, Mycobacterium marinum, Staphylococcus aureus, and Streptoccus pneumoniae. We will use these pathogens to determine how the infection dynamics change when the venom toxins are introduced into this model. At first we will be injecting a mix of purified secreted toxins from the insect-parasitic nematodes. Once we examine the properties of the mix as a whole, we may want to follow up on individual proteins from the toxin mix. To do this we would need to transfect E. coli with the gene of interest and purify recombinant toxin protein from E. coli to then inject into flies infected with one of the pathogens listed above. Infection dynamics will be studied by evaluating bacterial growth and fly survival.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
What are the objectives of the work with biohazardous materials: Determine the effect of fatty acid- and retinol-binding (FAR) toxins using an in vivo infection model. To better understand the role of secreted FAR proteins during parasitic nematode infections, we will utilize a Drosophila melanogaster infection model. The fly is an excellent model for human diseases and has conserved orthologs with >60% of single human disease genes. The innate immune pathways are also largely conserved between flies and humans. Infecting flies with different pathogens coupled with secreted nematode proteins will allow us to determine the specificity of the proteins as immune modulators. Hosts will be infected with a sub lethal dose of bacteria along with FAR toxins and the course of infection will be followed. We may also need to inject the flies with recombinant secreted FAR toxins, but we are using insect-parasitic nematodes so there is no health hazard with this part of the work. We will identify alterations to the infection outcome and the host immune response using health, bacterial growth, and immune reactivity (phagocytosis, anti-microbial peptide induction, and melanization). As we follow up on individual components of the toxic nematode secretions (insect-specific toxins), we will want to focus more in depth on certain individual toxins. To do that we will need to purify recombinant toxin proteins from transformed E. coli, and then inject those proteins into flies infected with bacteria. In terms of parasitic nematodes, we will be extracting nucleic acid for amplification and sequencing.
