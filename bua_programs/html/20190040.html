DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
The purpose of this project is to identify and characterize a type genetic element called a transposable element (jumping gene in popular literature). These elements move about the genome and create biological diversity in a species. We identify these elements using a combination of computer searches and wet-bench experiments. By studying these elements we may be able to use these elements to improve food crops or open new avenues to control mosquitos. We also characterize the genome of the Fairchild Mandarin and other plants studied by UCR professors and clone genes and promoters of classes of genes found in the circadian clock, host-pathogen interaction and fruit quality. We are studying genes that are important for growth, development, cell signaling and cellulose degradation in the nonpathogenic filamentous fungus and model organism Neurospora crassa in a project with UCR Professor Kathy Borkovich.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
Specifically new and interesting transposable elements will be cloned from plants and mosquitos into bacterial and yeast plasmid vectors. The cloned elements will be studied in yeast to identify elements that should be pursued as potential vectors for the host organism. Existing transgenic Arabidopsis plants are used in the class to demonstrate the requirements for transposable elements excision. Elements found to be active in yeast may be transformed into Arabidosis to further test their function. We will use reporter systems to test promoter activity requiring transformation of tobacco and citrus cultures with Agrobacterium. Genetic elements will be cloned into E. coli for sequencing and propagation of plasmid DNA. With Dr. Kathy Borkovich, we will use Escherichia coli vectors conferring resistance to ampicillin or chloramphenicol for cloning of Neurospora crassa DNA. N. crassa integrating vectors include those conferring hygromycin resistance (Aspergillus nidulans trpC promoter), BASTA resistance (A. nidulans trpC promoter ) and nourseothricin resistance (A. nidulans gpdA promoter). Recipients for transformation will be various N. crassa, S. cerevisiae and E. coli strains. N. crassa strains will be recipients for vectors conferring his-3 prototrophy (N. crassa gene) on N. crassa. S. cerevisiae vectors (2-micron or centromere-containing) include those conferring ura-3, trp-1 or leu-2 prototrophy for use in the two-hybrid assay or recombinational cloning of N. crassa gene fragments. Genes will include those involved in growth and development in N. crassa. All transfers/inoculations of N. crassa and S. cerevisiae will take place in a Laminar Airflow Workstation. N. crassa, S. cerevisiae and E. coli cultures will be autoclaved prior to disposal.

Define Controls
Do you need special equipment, engineering safeguards or personal protective equipment to maintain containment of your experiments (hoods, negative pressure rooms, gloves, etc.) True
If “yes”, list your recommendations. Transformed plants will be transferred to the professor's lab for proper growth conditions and containment. No transformed plants will be allowed to flower in the rooms listed on this application. Plants will be transferred according to the controls listed in the respective professor's approved BUA. All engineered strains will be autoclaved prior to disposal. Potential environmental or health hazards are considered minimal. Neurospora crassa, Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Escherichia coli are not plant pathogens. Certain strains of S. cerevisiae (not used in these experiments) have nominal impact as animal pathogens (and only in immunocompromised hosts). The strains of E. coli used are heavily mutated for genetic engineering approaches and considered minimal risk as animal pathogens.
Medical surveillance requirements or practices that you would recommend (antibody titering, etc.)? Not sure of LD50 of hygromycin B in humans. However, this drug is treated as toxic in the laboratory. It is dispensed in the biosafety cabinet using double-gloves and all transfer implements and media are immediately autoclaved after use. Two laminar flow hoods are already in the laboratory and will be used at all times when handling cultures of N. crassa. Gloves and required PPE will also be worn by personnel when handling cultures of E. coli, S. cerevisiae and N. crassa.
Suggested level of biological containment. BSL1
