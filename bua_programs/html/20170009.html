DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
This research focuses on understanding the developmental role of several families of plant transcription factors (TFs) that regulate various aspects of shoot architecture. Specific aspects of shoot architecture that we are interested in include meristem formation and maintenance, axillary branching, leaf shape, and leaf angle. Our research addresses the biological role of these TFs and the function of genes regulated by them. Physiological processes that are regulated by these TFs include the phytohormone brassinosteroid and light signaling. We work in Arabidopsis, maize and rice, and cowpea. We may use Nicotiana benthamiana for transient expression studies. We may work in tomato, Brachypodium distachyon, or other plants in the future.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
We will generate transgenic Arabidopsis plants that express either a wild type protein or a "dominant negative" protein (made by fusion to a strong transcriptional activation or repression domain), or a reporter gene under control of the ethanol inducible alcR transcription factor. Transgenic plants containing promoters of individual transcription factors (TFs) fused to either the GUS or GFP reporter gene will be generated. Downstream targets that are regulated by individual TFs are being identified and characterized. Transgenic rice plants that express enzymes that degrade brassinosteroid hormones, under a promoter that drives expression in the blade-sheath boundary have been generated to test the hypothesis that we can manipulate leaf angle by altering local brassinosteroid accumulation. We will use CRISPR-Cas9 for gene editiing to create targeted mutations in rice, Arabidopsis, and cowpea.
