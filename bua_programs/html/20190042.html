DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT
Provide a brief, non-technical description and purpose of the project.
Many human neurological diseases and disorders of the muscular system are caused by errors in the regulation of a process called alternative pre-mRNA splicing and mRNA decay. These include frontotemporal dementia, spinal muscular atrophy, some forms of autism, and various muscular dystrophies. We specifically study how alternative splicing and mRNA decay is regulated in the nervous system and the protein molecules that control it. These molecules are usually RNA binding proteins whose mutations have been implicated in various diseases. To understand how the processes are mis-regulated in diseases, one needs to first understand how they function in the normal organism and cells. We applied multidisciplinary techniques, including mouse genetics, functional genomics, informatics, neuroscience, molecular biology and cellular biology to understand how the RNA binding proteins recognize RNA, interact with one another, control RNA processing, affect cellular activities, brain development and functions. The understanding gained from these studies will be an essential first step in the development of treatments for a variety of human disorders.
What are the specific objectives of the work with biohazardous materials?
We generate recombinant DNA and viruses to induce or deplete gene expression in cells. We use bacteria to propagate DNA plasmids or make recombinant proteins. We use cell lines as an in vitro system and mouse as an in vivo system to investigate the roles of RNA binding proteins in mammalian cells.
